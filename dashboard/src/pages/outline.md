# Science 2028

## Outline

1. Overview
  - the promise of AI in science is great.
2. Challenges and limitations in science today
  - Reproduceability
  - Peer review delay
  - High barriers to entry
  - Lack of null result pulications
  - Lack of non-perfect confidence publications
3. Challenges, limitations and opportunities in science in the near future
  - Peer review collapse: 100x increase in scientific throughput without commensurate gains in review would lead to bottlenecks on publication rate.
  - AI needs faster signals: it takes months to have even modest results, draft pre-prints, go through peer review, publish at conferences, and have your work cited to know that it was impactful.
4. Desiging markets via microeconomics
5. Science as a marketplace of ideas
6. Conjecture/belief markets
  - how they work
    - all research aims to invalidate hypotheses about the world. 
  - What makes them different from event contracts/prediction markets? Conjecture/belief markets don't resolve.
  - What makes them different from stocks? They don't pay dividends.
7. Creating value in conjecture markets
  - Publishing evidence that yields delta in conjectures = impactful research.
    - Process: you buy related conjectures, publish your research, then benefit from the delta in price.
    - If we're able to correlate conjecture purchases from researchers before they publish their work, this gives us an implied posterior, and we can form Bayesian networks for beliefs through purchasing history.
  - Having a portfolio of conjectures which increases in value = science intuition.
8. Solving the cold-start problem
  - the cold-start problem: how do you get participants into your market if the market doesn't exist? 
  - answer: simulation backtesting on real historical participants' implied trading behavior.
  - Building a high-fidelity simulation = we can derive explicit alpha scores from research and portfolio values for scientists from their implied behavior. 
9. Portfolio value as RL signal for training better AI scientists
  - With a a high-fidelity simulation, we can assemble time series datasets and train agents to simulate trades in conjectures. This is how agents can learn an intution for what fields will be fruitful.
  - If we determine a way to estimate the cost of research papers, then we can train agents to maximize conjecture-alpha/unit-cost. This is how agents can learn to decide which experiments to pursue.
10. Onboarding real actors into the live market
  - With a high-fidelity simulation on historical data, we can start to onboard present actors with novel conjectures.
